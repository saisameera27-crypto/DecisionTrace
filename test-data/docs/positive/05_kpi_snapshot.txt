KPI SNAPSHOT: Q1 2024 Performance Review
Report Date: April 5, 2024
Reporting Period: Q1 2024 (Jan 1 - Mar 31, 2024)
Department: Product Engineering

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Q1 2024 showed mixed results. While we achieved 2 of 5 primary KPIs, we fell short on critical metrics including user growth and revenue targets.

KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS

1. MONTHLY ACTIVE USERS (MAU)
   Target: 500K
   Actual: 425K
   Status: ❌ MISSED (85% of target)
   Trend: +15% QoQ growth, but slower than projected
   Analysis: User acquisition costs increased 30%, conversion rates declined

2. REVENUE (ARR)
   Target: $5M ARR
   Actual: $4.2M ARR
   Status: ❌ MISSED (84% of target)
   Trend: +22% QoQ growth
   Analysis: Enterprise deals took longer to close than expected

3. CUSTOMER SATISFACTION (NPS)
   Target: 70
   Actual: 68
   Status: ⚠️ NEAR MISS (97% of target)
   Trend: +5 points QoQ improvement
   Analysis: Support response times improved, but feature requests backlogged

4. PRODUCT DEPLOYMENT FREQUENCY
   Target: 2 deployments per week
   Actual: 1.8 deployments per week
   Status: ✅ ACHIEVED (90% of target, acceptable)
   Trend: Consistent with previous quarter
   Analysis: Deployment process stable, minor improvements needed

5. MEAN TIME TO RECOVERY (MTTR)
   Target: < 30 minutes
   Actual: 22 minutes
   Status: ✅ ACHIEVED (73% of target, exceeded)
   Trend: Improved from 35 minutes last quarter
   Analysis: Incident response procedures working well

DECISION POINTS
Based on these results, leadership made the following decisions:

DECISION 1: Adjust Q2 Targets
- Reduced MAU target from 600K to 550K (more realistic)
- Maintained revenue target at $6M (aggressive but achievable)
- Rationale: Q1 performance indicates need for more conservative growth projections

DECISION 2: Increase Marketing Budget
- Approved additional $500K for user acquisition
- Focus on high-converting channels identified in Q1 analysis
- Rationale: User growth is critical for long-term success

DECISION 3: Accelerate Enterprise Sales
- Hired 2 additional enterprise sales reps
- Implemented new sales enablement tools
- Rationale: Enterprise deals drive revenue but take longer to close

RISKS IDENTIFIED
1. If user growth doesn't accelerate, we may miss annual targets
2. Increased marketing spend may reduce profitability
3. Enterprise sales ramp-up may take longer than expected

NEXT STEPS
- Review Q2 progress mid-quarter (May 15)
- Adjust strategy if KPIs continue to lag
- Consider additional cost-cutting measures if revenue doesn't improve
